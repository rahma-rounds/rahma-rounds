Here you can find all kinds of pre-med resources from Free MCAT Practice Exams to and Admissions timeline. Explore this page to find resources that can help you on your journey to become a physician!

Undergrad Timeline

Freshman Year
Here are the resources you might find useful as a freshman. There is no need to use every link. Just browse around to find what you might find useful.

Fall Semester

Start your most basic science general classes (General Biology and Chemistry). Explore to find which pre-med extracurricular at your school and in your local area. Join pre-med club, start volunteering in a healthcare setting (hospital, clinic, etc.), seek research entry level opportunities

Spring Semester

You will likely want to continue with the second semester of you general science courses. This semester is where you might want to nail down your extracurricular activities and maybe seek leadership activities.

Sophomore Year
Here are the resources you might find useful as a freshman. There is no need to use every link. Just browse around to find what you might find useful.

Fall Semester

Courses: Organic Chemistry I with Lab, Physics I with Lab, Biochemistry, Psychology or Sociology (whichever wasn't taken first year). Activities: Continue research, shadow physicians, prepare for summer research or internship programs.

Spring Semester

Courses: Organic Chemistry II with Lab, Physics II with Lab, Advanced Biology (Genetics, Microbiology, etc.), Humanities or Social Science elective. Activities: Continue volunteering and shadowing, start studying for the MCAT, apply for summer programs (research, clinical internships, etc.).
