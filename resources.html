Undergrad Timeline

Freshman Year
Here are the resources you might find useful as a freshman. Within each description, you can click on links to be taken to specific section of the site. Browse around to find what you might find useful!

Fall Semester

Courses: General Chemistry I with Lab, Biology I with Lab, Calculus or Statistics, English Composition.

Activities: Join pre-med club, start volunteering in a healthcare setting (hospital, clinic, etc.), seek research opportunities.

Spring Semester

Courses: General Chemistry II with Lab, Biology II with Lab, Introduction to Psychology or Sociology, English or Humanities.

Activities: Continue volunteering, get involved in extracurricular activities, seek leadership positions.

Sophomore Year
Here are the resources you might find useful as a sophomore. Within each description, you can click on links to be taken to specific section of the site. Browse around to find what you might find useful!

Fall Semester

Courses: Organic Chemistry I with Lab, Physics I with Lab, Biochemistry, Psychology or Sociology (whichever wasn't taken first year). Activities: Continue research, shadow physicians, prepare for summer research or internship programs.

Spring Semester

Courses: Organic Chemistry II with Lab, Physics II with Lab, Advanced Biology (Genetics, Microbiology, etc.), Humanities or Social Science elective. Activities: Continue volunteering and shadowing, start studying for the MCAT, apply for summer programs (research, clinical internships, etc.).

Volunteer in Different Departments at UI Health
UI Health Chicago

A wide array of departments need volunteers. Here are some examples: greeters and way finders, musicians, diagnostics & imaging services, cancer center, physical/occupational therapy, outpatient pediatrics

Special Services Volunteer at RUSH
RUSH University Medical Center

RUSH's volunteers have a variety of roles including: Greeters and escorts, Clerical support, Delivering flowers, mail, specimens and crafts, Patient relations including providing companionship, and Patient care areas and the emergency department.
